Abstract

The World Happiness index was recently developed to show how a nation’s happiness is determined by certain socio-economic, health and political factors. This new index is now being used by policy makers, governments and other stakeholders in making national decisions. The sub-indicators that sum up to make the Happiness index includes GDP Per Capita, social support, healthy life expectancy, freedom to make life choices, perception of corruption and generosity. The Sustainable Development Index is calculated annually to show how countries are progressing towards the achievement of the sustainable development goals by 2030. This project uses several visualizations to show the relationship between countries’ level of development and the happiness of their citizens. From the analysis of data, it was found that countries that were in the 10th percentile in the sustainable development ranking also had the highest levels of happiness. This technical report will discuss the objectives of the project, methodology and findings from the analysis.

Objective of Project

This project aims to visually show the relationship between development and happiness. The objective of the project is group countries by happiness index and sustainable development index and also show the trend in the sub-indicators between 2015 and 2019. The happiness index will also be compared to a country’s sustainable development index. The sub-indicators analyzed are GDP Per Capita, social support, healthy life expectancy, freedom to make life choices, perception of corruption and generosity.
